The Book

Under Every Rock Turned

A Case for Christ Through the Life of a Skeptic

My parents introduced me to God and Santa Claus with the same voice and the same certainty. I was four years old. When Santa fell, God went with him.

What followed was thirty years — through my parents' divorce, a childhood built on nothing, a bunk in Hutchins State Jail where Michael Behe's Darwin's Black Box landed in my hands, through Narcotics Anonymous (clean since December 8, 2008), into a Christian motorcycle club where I made the case for God in recovery rooms while refusing to call myself a Christian — until a phone call with Dr. James Tour where I finally ran out of reasons to keep denying what every rock had been pointing toward.

No book in the Christian apologetics canon combines addiction, prison, scientific argument, and a genuine atheist-to-Christian conversion told in a street-level voice. This is that book.

The Author

Jonathan Stephens is the owner of Stephens Advanced LLC, a fire suppression company in the Dallas–Fort Worth area. He has been clean since December 8, 2008.

He spent years making the intellectual case for God in recovery rooms and church settings before he would call himself a Christian. Under Every Rock Turned is the record of why that changed.

He has no platform. No social media following. No prior publications. What he has is a story that made James Tour — a man who says he never writes endorsements for people outside his academic circle — read the entire manuscript in one night and write back before morning.
